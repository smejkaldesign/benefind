## TL;DR

The State Trade Expansion Program (STEP) gives small businesses up to **$5,000-$15,000** in reimbursement for export activities — trade shows, website localization, international marketing, and more. It's SBA-funded but administered through states, so availability depends on your state having an active grant cycle. You contribute a 25% cash match; the program covers the rest. If your business sells products or services with 51%+ U.S. content and you're ready to go international, STEP is one of the lowest-friction export funding options available.

## What STEP is

The State Trade Expansion Program was created by the Small Business Jobs Act of 2010 (Section 22 of the Small Business Act, 15 USC Section 649). The SBA awards grants to state and territorial governments, which then make sub-awards directly to eligible small businesses.

Think of it as a pass-through: the federal government funds states, and states fund small business export activities. The SBA allocated approximately **$18-20 million annually** to states, with individual state awards ranging from $100,000 to $900,000 per year depending on state size and export activity. From those state pools, businesses typically receive **$5,000-$15,000 per grant cycle**.

The program's goal is straightforward: help small businesses enter or expand into international markets by covering a meaningful portion of the real costs involved — trade show participation, international marketing, website translation, and other front-end export expenses that often prevent businesses from making the leap.

### How the money flows

```
SBA → State Trade Office → Eligible Small Business
```

Because funding flows through states, the experience varies considerably by state. Some states run highly organized programs with clear deadlines and published application windows. Others distribute funds on a first-come, first-served basis until the annual award runs out. **The most important first step is always to contact your state's STEP administrator.**

## Eligible activities

STEP covers a specific list of export-related activities. You can mix and match activities within a single grant cycle.

### Trade shows and exhibitions

International trade show booth fees, travel expenses, and display materials are the most commonly funded activity. If you're heading to a trade show like CES, Natural Products Expo, or an industry-specific international show, STEP can cover a significant portion of costs.

Eligible expenses typically include:
- Booth rental fees
- Travel and lodging for the international component
- Exhibit display materials and shipping
- Translation of marketing materials for the show

### Website localization

Adapting your website for international markets — including translation, currency conversion, international checkout, and country-specific SEO — is one of STEP's most cost-effective eligible activities. A basic website localization project that might cost $8,000-$12,000 becomes a $2,000-$3,000 out-of-pocket expense after STEP reimbursement.

Eligible expenses include:
- Foreign language translation of website content
- Website design adaptations for target markets
- International e-commerce functionality
- Search engine optimization for foreign markets

### International marketing

Designing and placing marketing materials targeting foreign markets — digital ads, social media campaigns, international directory listings, and content marketing for export audiences — qualifies under STEP.

### Foreign trade missions

Organized business delegations visiting foreign markets are eligible, particularly SBA-organized missions or state-sponsored trade missions.

### Export B2B matchmaking

Services that connect you with pre-qualified foreign buyers or partners are covered, including U.S. Department of Commerce Gold Key Service (pre-arranged meetings with foreign prospects), International Partner Search, and International Company Profile services.

### Export training and compliance

Courses, seminars, and workshops on exporting (logistics, customs, trade finance, documentation) qualify. International compliance testing and certifications for products entering foreign markets are also eligible.

## Funding amounts and the 25% match

STEP is a **reimbursement-based grant**, not an upfront payment. You pay for the export activities first, then submit documentation for reimbursement. The program covers **up to 75% of eligible project costs**, and you contribute at least 25% in cash.

**Key matching rules:**
- Match must be cash, not in-kind contributions
- Match must be from company funds, not other federal grant funds
- You submit receipts and documentation after activities are complete

**Example:** You participate in a trade show with $10,000 in eligible expenses. STEP reimburses $7,500. Your out-of-pocket cost: $2,500.

**Typical award range:** $5,000-$15,000 per business per grant cycle. A few states with larger state pools award up to $25,000 for specific export initiatives.

### Stacking with other programs

STEP can be combined with other export assistance programs, but you cannot use STEP to reimburse expenses that are already funded by another federal grant. Useful stacking combinations include:

- **EXIM Bank export financing** for the underlying transactions
- **USDA Market Access Program (MAP)** if you're in agriculture
- **U.S. Commercial Service** trade promotion resources (many are free)
- **State-level export assistance** programs on top of STEP

## Eligibility requirements

### Business requirements

| Requirement | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Business type | For-profit small business per SBA size standards |
| Operating history | At least 1 year in operation in the U.S. |
| U.S. content | 51% or more U.S. content in goods or services |
| Federal standing | Not debarred or suspended from federal programs |
| SAM.gov | Must be registered |
| Cash match | Must contribute at least 25% in cash |

### Export readiness

"Export readiness" means you have a product or service that's genuinely suitable for international markets — not that you've already exported. States want to see:
- A specific target market or region (not just "global")
- A realistic export plan or at least clear export goals
- Evidence the product/service can meet foreign market requirements

First-time exporters are eligible and often prioritized. STEP is explicitly designed to help businesses take the first step into international markets.

### Who doesn't qualify

- Businesses located in states without an active STEP award (check state availability first)
- Agricultural producers (see USDA MAP and VAPG programs instead)
- Businesses selling products with less than 51% U.S. content
- Businesses currently suspended or debarred from federal programs
- Organizations without SAM.gov registration

## How to apply

### Step 1: Check state availability

STEP funds flow through states, and not every state has an active award in every year. Go to SBA.gov and search "STEP awardees" for the current directory of states with active STEP awards. Alternatively, contact your state's economic development office, trade office, or Small Business Development Center (SBDC) to ask whether STEP is available.

**Important for 2026:** The FY 2025-2026 grant cycle was delayed due to a federal government shutdown that prevented SBA from notifying states of awards before the disruption. Contact your state STEP office for current availability and expected timeline.

### Step 2: Contact your state STEP administrator

Every state with a STEP award has a designated administrator, typically within the state's trade or economic development office. Contact them before applying — they'll tell you:
- Current funding availability (funds are often distributed first-come, first-served)
- State-specific eligibility requirements (some states have additional requirements)
- Which activities are currently reimbursable under the state's grant terms
- The application timeline and deadline

### Step 3: Prepare your application

State applications vary, but you'll typically need:
- Business registration and proof of 1+ year in operation
- Documentation of SBA size standard compliance
- Written export plan or statement of export goals
- Target market(s) you're planning to enter
- List of planned export activities with cost estimates
- Proof of 51%+ U.S. content in your products/services
- SAM.gov registration
- Plan for contributing the 25% cash match

### Step 4: Get approval, then execute

STEP is a reimbursement program — you must receive approval before incurring expenses in most states. Don't book trade shows or start website projects until your application is approved. After approval, execute the activities, collect receipts and documentation, and submit your reimbursement request.

### Step 5: Submit reimbursement request

Submit receipts, invoices, and activity documentation according to the state's reimbursement process. Turnaround times vary; expect 4-8 weeks for payment.

### Timeline overview

| Step | Typical Timeline |
|---|---|
| State availability check | 1-2 days |
| Application preparation | 1-2 weeks |
| State review and approval | 2-6 weeks |
| Execute export activities | 1-6 months |
| Reimbursement processing | 4-8 weeks after submission |

## State-by-state overview

Each state runs its STEP program slightly differently. Here's what to expect in major states:

**California:** Administered through CalOSBA. Application typically opens in fall, funds distributed until exhausted. Tech and manufacturing companies are common recipients. Supplement with CA GO-Biz trade promotion resources.

**Texas:** Administered through the Office of the Governor Economic Development & Tourism. Works alongside TexasOne private sector export assistance and Governor's Office trade missions.

**New York:** Administered through Empire State Development's international trade programs. Strong support for manufacturing and agricultural exporters.

**Florida:** Administered through Enterprise Florida (trade promotion arm). Coordinate with Florida Export Directory and trade missions organized by Enterprise Florida.

**Pennsylvania:** Administered through PA Department of Community & Economic Development (DCED). Works alongside DCED's international business development programs.

**Ohio:** Administered through Ohio Development Services Agency. Often combined with Ohio's own export assistance resources.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does every state have STEP funding?

No. The SBA awards grants competitively to states, and not every state applies or receives an award every year. Additionally, federal budget disruptions can delay or reduce state awards. Always verify with your state office before planning activities around STEP funding.

### Can I use STEP for domestic trade shows?

No. STEP is specifically for export activities — international trade shows, foreign market marketing, website localization for foreign audiences, etc. Domestic trade shows and activities targeting only U.S. customers don't qualify.

### What if I'm a service business, not a product company?

Service businesses qualify if their service contains 51% or more U.S. content. Many service businesses — software companies, consulting firms, designers, educators — successfully use STEP to fund international marketing and website localization. The key is demonstrating that your service can be delivered to international customers.

### Can I apply if I've never exported before?

Yes. STEP is designed to help businesses enter export markets for the first time. You don't need prior export experience, just a product or service that can be sold internationally and a plan for doing so.

### What's the difference between STEP and a trade loan?

STEP is a grant — you don't repay it. Trade loans (like EXIM Bank financing) help you fund the underlying export transactions. They serve different purposes and can be used together.

### How long does the application process take?

From first contact with your state administrator to receiving reimbursement, expect 3-6 months total. The approval process typically takes 2-6 weeks; then you execute your activities; then you wait 4-8 weeks for reimbursement. Plan your export activities timeline with these lead times in mind.

### Can I apply for STEP multiple years in a row?

Yes, in most states. STEP is an annual program and businesses can apply in successive grant cycles. Some states limit the number of consecutive years or require a waiting period between awards.

## The bottom line

STEP won't fund your entire export expansion, but it meaningfully reduces the cost of the activities that matter most at the front end — trade show participation and website localization are typically where businesses realize the best ROI on STEP dollars.

The 25% cash match requirement is intentional: the program rewards businesses that are genuinely committed to exporting, not just testing the waters with zero skin in the game.

Start by calling your state STEP administrator. They can tell you in 10 minutes whether funds are available, what the current application window looks like, and what activities your state is prioritizing.
