## TL;DR

LIHEAP — the **Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program** — helps about 6 million U.S. households pay their heating and cooling bills every year. Average benefit is around **$500 per household**, but crisis grants can be much higher. Most states open applications in **October or November** and keep them open through March or April or until funds run out. This guide walks through exactly who qualifies, what documents you need, and how to apply step by step.

## What LIHEAP actually pays for

LIHEAP is federally funded but run through state and tribal agencies, which means the details vary. At the broadest level, LIHEAP can help pay for:

- **Heating bills** in winter (gas, electric, oil, propane, wood)
- **Cooling bills** in summer in participating states (mostly hot-climate ones)
- **Energy crisis assistance** — emergency utility shutoff prevention, fuel delivery when tanks run dry, broken furnace repair or replacement
- **Weatherization referrals** — many LIHEAP applications automatically enroll you in the Weatherization Assistance Program for free insulation and window upgrades

Benefits are usually paid **directly to the utility company** on your behalf, not to you. You'll see it as a credit on your next bill.

## Who qualifies for LIHEAP in 2026

Federal rules set the maximum income at the higher of:

- **150% of the federal poverty level**, or
- **60% of the state median income**

States can set their own cutoffs within those bounds. Most choose 150% FPL, but about 15 states use the higher 60% state median income number — those are the most generous.

### Automatic eligibility (categorical)

You **automatically qualify** for LIHEAP in most states if you or anyone in your household receives:

- SNAP (food stamps)
- TANF
- SSI
- Certain veterans benefits
- Some states include Medicaid

If you qualify through one of these, you can skip the income documentation step of the application. You still have to apply, but approval is much faster.

## How much can you actually get

LIHEAP benefits vary wildly by state, household size, income, and the type of fuel you use. As a rough guide:

- **Standard heating benefit**: $200 to $1,500 per household per winter
- **Crisis benefit** (utility shutoff, broken furnace, empty tank): additional $500 to $5,000
- **Cooling benefit** (where offered): $200 to $800

Alaska, Vermont, Maine, and upstate New York tend to pay the most because their heating needs are the highest.

## When to apply — and why timing matters

LIHEAP is a **first-come, first-served program with fixed state funding**. If you wait until February to apply, funds might already be exhausted. Here's the typical schedule:

- **October to mid-November**: Most states open applications. Priority groups (elderly, disabled, households with young children) often get to apply a few weeks earlier.
- **November to January**: Peak application season. Funds are still available in most states.
- **February to April**: Standard applications increasingly denied for funding reasons. Crisis applications still accepted.
- **May to September**: Cooling assistance applications in participating states.

**The best time to apply is within the first two weeks of your state opening applications** — usually late October or early November. Mark your calendar.

## Documents you'll need

Gather these before you start the application to avoid back-and-forth delays:

- **Photo ID** for the head of household (driver's license, state ID, passport)
- **Social Security cards** (or ITIN documentation) for every household member, or the numbers written on your application
- **Proof of residence** (lease, mortgage statement, property tax bill)
- **Recent utility bills** — most critical. They want to see:
  - Your most recent heating fuel bill (gas, electric, oil, propane)
  - The account number
  - Whether you have a past-due balance or shutoff notice
- **Proof of income** for the last 30 days for everyone 18+ in the household:
  - Paystubs
  - Self-employment records
  - Social Security award letter
  - SSI or SSDI award letter
  - Pension or retirement statements
  - Child support statements
  - Unemployment benefit letters
- **Proof of SNAP/TANF/SSI** if applying through categorical eligibility
- **Rental subsidy documentation** if you receive Section 8 or live in subsidized housing (sometimes LIHEAP benefit is reduced if your rent already includes utilities)

## Step-by-step: how to apply

### Step 1: Find your state LIHEAP agency

Every state runs LIHEAP through a different agency — usually Human Services, Community Action Agencies, or sometimes the state energy office. The easiest way to find yours:

- Visit **acf.hhs.gov/ocs/low-income-home-energy-assistance-program-liheap** and click the "Find Local Help" map, or
- Call **211** from any phone
- Call the **National Energy Assistance Referral line: 1-866-674-6327**

Most states now have online applications. A few still require paper or in-person. Some states let you apply through any community action agency office, which can be easier if you need help filling out the form.

### Step 2: Gather your documents

See the list above. Missing documents are the top reason applications get delayed. If you're missing something, apply anyway and submit it within the grace period they give you.

### Step 3: Fill out the application

The application is usually 4–8 pages. Common fields:

- Household composition (everyone living at the address)
- Monthly income from every source for every adult
- Housing costs (rent or mortgage)
- Heating fuel type and utility account numbers
- Whether you're in a crisis situation (past-due bill, shutoff notice, empty fuel tank, broken heating system)

**If you have a shutoff notice or your tank is empty, mark the crisis box.** This moves you to the front of the queue for expedited review, usually within 48 hours.

### Step 4: Submit and confirm

Submit online, by mail, or in person. If submitting online, save the confirmation number. If submitting by mail, send it certified mail so you have proof of delivery.

Most states will send a confirmation letter within a week saying your application was received. If you don't hear anything after 10 days, call and check.

### Step 5: Processing and approval

Standard applications are processed in **15 to 45 days**. Crisis applications in **48 hours or less** in most states.

You'll get a decision letter telling you:

- Whether you're approved
- How much your benefit is
- Which account it's being applied to
- Whether you need to do anything else (sign a utility authorization, etc.)

### Step 6: See the credit on your bill

Your utility company will apply the LIHEAP benefit as a credit on your next bill. This is the part people most often miss — **the money doesn't come to you directly**. It goes to the utility.

## Crisis assistance — the fast-track version

LIHEAP's crisis component is one of the most important and most underused parts of the program. If any of these apply, you qualify for fast-track crisis assistance:

- You have a **shutoff notice** from your utility
- Your utility has **already been shut off**
- Your fuel tank is **empty or below 25%**
- Your heating system is **broken and needs repair or replacement**
- You **can't afford a delivery** that's scheduled

Crisis applications are processed within **18 to 48 hours** in most states. The crisis benefit is separate from (and in addition to) the standard benefit, so you can get both.

## What LIHEAP does NOT cover

- Late fees or reconnection fees (in most states)
- Bills already paid
- Second homes or vacation properties
- Businesses or commercial accounts
- Vehicle fuel (gasoline for cars)
- Pellet stoves or wood stoves in some states (check your state's rules)

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I have to reapply every year?

Yes. LIHEAP benefits are annual. You'll need to submit a new application each heating season.

### Can I get LIHEAP if I rent an apartment?

Yes. If heat is included in your rent, you may still qualify for a reduced benefit in some states. If you pay utilities separately, you apply with your own utility bills.

### What if I live in public housing?

You can still apply. Benefits may be reduced because heating is often included, but crisis assistance is still available.

### Can I get LIHEAP and SNAP at the same time?

Yes. They're separate programs. In fact, receiving SNAP may make you automatically eligible for LIHEAP in your state.

### What if my application is denied?

You have the right to appeal. Most states give you 30 days to file an appeal. Denials for missing documentation are easy to reverse — just submit the missing paperwork.

### What if I owe money to the utility from a prior year?

Most LIHEAP funds can be applied to current and recent past-due balances. Some very old balances may not be covered, but you should apply anyway and let the agency decide.

### Can I use LIHEAP to pay for a new furnace?

In some states, yes — through the crisis/emergency component. Others handle furnace replacement through the Weatherization Assistance Program. Ask your LIHEAP caseworker which program handles it.
